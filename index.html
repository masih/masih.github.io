Masih Hajiarab Derkani

I am a software engineer at Adobe, business platform services. I currently work on a highly resilient distributed message dissemination system with guaranteed sequential consistency and at-least-once delivery.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral research fellow at the university of St Andrews, school of Computer Science. I worked with Dr. Graham Kirby and Prof. Alan Dearle on Digitising Scotland, a project constructing a linked genealogy of 24 million Scottish historical records since 1855.

Prior to that, I worked on Qraqrbox, a university spinout company providing a distributed delay-tolerant content delivery platform powered by renewable energies.

In my PhD, I investigated algorithmic adaptation of routing state maintenance in structured Peer-to-Peer (P2P) overlays, where the scope of adaptation is enlarged to include the maintenance algorithm itself rather than being limited to the parameter values of a single algorithm. I confirmed the hypothesis that a Key-based Routing (KBR) system can automatically and dynamically adapt the structure of its maintenance algorithm to changing environments, and can do so in a fully decentralised manner without any human intervention, and outperform static KBR systems such as Chord in terms of performance and resource consumption, i.e. lookup correctness, lookup latency and bandwidth usage. I had the privilege of completing my PhD under the supervision of Dr. Graham Kirby. My work was generously funded by Scottish Informatics and Computer Science Alliance (SICSA).

Prior to my PhD, I received my MSc in “Advanced Computer Science” from the university of St. Andrews. My Master’s thesis, titled “Trombone: A Zero-Hop, Zero-Configuration Peer-to-Peer Routing Algorithm”, was supervised by Prof. Alan Dearle, which led to my PhD studies. I was kindly awarded the 2010 Master’s class medal.

My research interests include distributed systems, P2P systems, software architecture/engineering, autonomic computing and unsupervised machine learning.
